10 Best Gyms in Jacksonville FL [2026]

The definitive guide to finding your perfect gym in Duval County.

Jacksonville is one of the fastest-growing cities in the Southeast, and its fitness scene has kept pace. Whether you're looking for a budget-friendly chain, a hardcore powerlifting gym, or a full-service fitness center with personal training and recovery amenities, Duval has options. We put together this guide to help you compare the best gyms in Jacksonville FL so you can find the right fit for your goals.

2. Planet Fitness Jacksonville

Multiple locations throughout Jacksonville

Best for: Budget-conscious beginners, casual cardio

Planet Fitness is the most affordable gym option in Jacksonville, with memberships starting at $10/month. They have numerous locations across the city, from the Southside and Arlington to the Beaches and Northside. Planet Fitness is known for its "Judgement Free Zone" philosophy and is a solid choice for people who want basic cardio machines and a clean environment.

However, Planet Fitness Jacksonville locations typically don't have free-weight barbells, squat racks, or heavy dumbbells. If your goal is strength training, powerlifting, or bodybuilding, you'll quickly outgrow what Planet Fitness offers. The "lunk alarm" policy also discourages serious lifting, which makes it a poor fit for intermediate or advanced athletes.

3. Anytime Fitness Jacksonville

Multiple locations throughout Jacksonville

Best for: Convenience, 24/7 access, travelers

Anytime Fitness has several Jacksonville locations and offers true 24/7 access with a key fob entry system. Memberships typically run $40-50/month. The equipment selection is better than Planet Fitness but still limited compared to dedicated strength gyms. Most Anytime Fitness Jacksonville locations have a few squat racks, a Smith machine, dumbbells, and a decent selection of machines.

The biggest advantage of Anytime Fitness is the nationwide network — your membership works at any location in the country. The downside is that individual locations are relatively small, and during peak hours the limited equipment can mean waiting for popular stations.

4. LA Fitness Jacksonville

Locations on Southside Blvd and other areas

Best for: Group classes, pool access, general fitness

LA Fitness offers a traditional big-box gym experience with a decent free weight area, machines, group fitness classes, a pool, and basketball courts at most Jacksonville locations. Memberships usually start around $35/month. It's a well-rounded option for people who want variety, though the free weight areas can feel crowded during peak hours.

LA Fitness is a solid middle-of-the-road choice, but like most large chains, the equipment is geared toward general fitness rather than serious strength training. Don't expect specialty bars, competition racks, or a powerlifting-friendly atmosphere.

5. EOS Fitness Jacksonville

Expanding into the Jacksonville market

Best for: Modern amenities, group fitness, affordability

EOS Fitness has been expanding rapidly across the Southeast and has opened locations in the Jacksonville area. They offer a modern gym experience with group classes, personal training, a pool at select locations, and a clean, well-maintained environment. Pricing is competitive, typically in the $10-25/month range depending on the tier.

EOS is a strong option for general fitness, but like most mid-range chains, the strength training equipment is standard commercial-grade rather than competition-quality. If your focus is powerlifting or bodybuilding, a specialty gym will serve you better.

6. Chuze Fitness Jacksonville

Newer to the Jacksonville market

Best for: Budget-friendly with more amenities than Planet Fitness

Chuze Fitness has been growing in the Jacksonville area and offers a step up from the ultra-budget chains. Memberships are affordable, and Chuze locations typically feature a cinema room, group classes, team training, and a reasonably equipped free weight area. It's a good option for people who want more than Planet Fitness but don't need the intensity of a dedicated strength gym.

7. Florida Extreme

Best for: Combat sports, functional training

Florida Extreme is a local Jacksonville gym that focuses on combat sports, MMA, and functional training alongside traditional gym equipment. If you're looking for a gym that combines martial arts training with weight lifting, Florida Extreme offers that hybrid experience. The atmosphere tends to be more intense than the average chain gym.

8. The Gym Jax (Dynamic Fitness)

Best for: Local independent gym experience

The Gym Jax, also known as Dynamic Fitness, is another locally-owned option in the Jacksonville gym scene. As an independent facility, they can offer a more personalized experience than the big chains. The gym serves a mix of general fitness members and more serious lifters.

9. Lift365

Best for: Local fitness community

Lift365 is a local Jacksonville gym that has built a following among fitness enthusiasts in the area. As an independent gym, Lift365 offers an alternative to the franchise model with a community-focused approach. If you're looking for gyms beyond the big chains, Lift365 is worth checking out.

10. Workout Anytime Jacksonville

Best for: Budget 24/7 access

Workout Anytime is a franchise that offers 24/7 access at a budget price point. Jacksonville has several locations, typically in strip malls with a compact footprint. The equipment selection is basic but functional, with a mix of machines, free weights, and cardio. It's a step above Planet Fitness for weight training but well below dedicated strength facilities.

How to Choose the Right Gym in Jacksonville

With so many options, choosing a gym comes down to a few key factors:

  • Your goals: If you're serious about strength, powerlifting, or bodybuilding, choose a gym built for that. Chain gyms are designed for general fitness and often lack the equipment and atmosphere that dedicated lifters need.
  • Equipment quality: There's a huge difference between commercial-grade machines and competition-quality racks and bars. Serious lifters can feel the difference immediately.
  • Community: The people you train around matter. A gym where everyone is pushing hard creates a different training environment than a gym designed around casual drop-ins.
  • Access hours: If you train early morning or late at night, 24/7 access is essential. Not all gyms offer this.
  • Additional services: Personal training, massage therapy, recovery amenities — these can make a big difference in your results and recovery.
